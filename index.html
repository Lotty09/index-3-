Spaghetti Carbonara

Spaghetti Carbonara is a classic Italian pasta dish made with eggs, cheese, pancetta, and pepper. It's a simple yet delicious meal perfect for any occasion.

Ingredients

  • 200g spaghetti
  • 100g pancetta or bacon
  • 2 large eggs
  • 50g grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Optional: Chopped parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the spaghetti according to package instructions, until al dente.
  2. Meanwhile, heat the olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the pancetta and cook until crispy, about 5 minutes. Remove the pancetta and set aside.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs and Parmesan cheese.
  4. Once the pasta is cooked, reserve 1/2 cup of pasta water. Drain the spaghetti and quickly toss it with the egg mixture off the heat, adding reserved pasta water a little at a time to create a creamy sauce.
  5. Add the crispy pancetta back into the pasta, stirring to combine. Season with salt and plenty of black pepper to taste.
  6. Serve immediately, garnished with parsley if desired. Enjoy!
  7. Add heavy cream for a richer sauce. For a more authentic version, avoid cream and stick to eggs and cheese.

Note: Make sure to mix the egg mixture with the hot pasta off the heat to prevent the eggs from scrambling.
